Reading — Strategies

Use skimming to find the main idea and scanning for specific facts. Practice matching headings and True/False/Not Given.

Short passage

Passage: Urban gardening has become popular in recent years. Residents convert small spaces like balconies and rooftops into vegetable plots. These gardens reduce food miles and provide fresh produce, especially in dense cities where access to green space is limited.

Questions

  1. Urban gardening helps reduce the distance food travels. (True/False/Not Given)
  2. Only large public parks are suitable for growing vegetables. (True/False/Not Given)
  3. Urban gardening is popular because it increases access to fresh produce in cities. (True/False/Not Given)
✓ Show answers
  1. True — the passage says these gardens reduce food miles.
  2. False — the passage says small spaces like balconies and rooftops are used.
  3. True — the passage explicitly states it provides fresh produce in dense cities.

Longer passage — Tourism & Local Communities

Passage: Over the past two decades, tourism has become a major source of income for many small towns. While increased visitor numbers bring money to local businesses, they sometimes cause overcrowding, strain public services and raise living costs for residents. Some communities manage tourism by limiting the number of new hotels and supporting locally-owned businesses to ensure benefits remain in the area.

Questions (choose the best answer)

  1. According to the passage, tourism can cause which problem?
    • A. Improved public transport
    • B. Lower living costs
    • C. Overcrowding
    • D. Decreased visitor numbers
  2. What solution is mentioned to help keep benefits local?
    • A. Building more airports
    • B. Limiting new hotels
    • C. Reducing taxes for tourists
    • D. Banning local businesses
  3. True/False/Not Given: Tourism always reduces living costs for residents.
  4. Match: Which phrase matches 'ensure benefits remain in the area'?
    • A. Keep profits within the community
    • B. Encourage people to move away
    • C. Increase visitor spending abroad
  5. What is the overall tone of the passage?
    • A. Entirely positive
    • B. Completely negative
    • C. Balanced — discussing both benefits and problems
Answers
  1. C — Overcrowding is explicitly mentioned.
  2. B — Limiting the number of new hotels is given as an example.
  3. False — the passage says tourism can raise living costs (not reduce).
  4. A — "Keep profits within the community" matches the idea.
  5. C — The passage balances both benefits and problems.
