Writing — Task 1 & Task 2

Task 1 (Graph)

Describe the main trends and compare where relevant. Write an overview first.

Exercise (Task 1): The chart shows smartphone usage in Country X: 2005 — 10%, 2010 — 35%, 2015 — 70%, 2020 — 85%. Write a 150-word summary describing the main trends.

✓ Checklist for a good Task 1
  • Include an overview (major trend)
  • Highlight specific data points and comparisons
  • Avoid personal opinion

Task 2 (Essay)

Plan (intro, 2 body paragraphs, conclusion). Support ideas with examples and be clear about your position.

✓ Sample Task 2 prompt

Some people think that unpaid community service should be a compulsory part of high school education. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

Sample approach: Give your opinion, provide two supporting paragraphs (benefits of civic skills and practical experience), then conclude.

Quick practice

Spend 40 minutes on Task 2: plan 5 minutes, write 30 minutes, review 5 minutes.

Model planning outline (Task 2)

Intro — state agreement; Body 1 — developing responsibility and teamwork; Body 2 — real-world skills and employability; Conclusion — restate position and suggest optional alternatives.

Model answers

Task 1 — Model summary (150 words)

The chart illustrates the percentage of people using smartphones in Country X between 2005 and 2020. Overall, smartphone ownership rose substantially throughout the period. In 2005 approximately 10% of the population used smartphones; this figure climbed to 35% by 2010 and reached 70% in 2015, before increasing more moderately to 85% in 2020. The most significant growth occurred between 2010 and 2015 when usage nearly doubled. Although growth slowed after 2015, by 2020 a clear majority of the population owned smartphones. This trend suggests rapid adoption during the early 2010s followed by market saturation in the latter half of the period.

Task 2 — Sample short essay (band-style)

Many believe that unpaid community service should be mandatory in high schools. I partly agree with this view. Introducing service as a required activity can build civic responsibility and give students experience of teamwork and real-world problems. For example, volunteering at local care homes teaches empathy and communication skills, which are useful later in life. However, making it compulsory could disadvantage students who must work part-time or care for family members. A better approach is to offer structured, credited community-service programmes with flexibility or alternatives such as school-based projects. This maintains benefits while accommodating different circumstances. In conclusion, community service is valuable and should be strongly encouraged, but mandatory requirements should include exceptions and flexible options to ensure fairness.
